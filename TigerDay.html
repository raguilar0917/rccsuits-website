Tiger Day

About

Riverside City College is excited to announce its first Annual Tiger Programming Competition and Educational Event. This event is intended for K-12 students who are eager to learn about the wonders of technology and how programming relates their daily lives. This programming event will broaden their interests and expand their view and career path options in the diverse field of technology.

Elementary Students

Elementary School students will learn about the wonders of technology and how programming has affected our daily lives. Introducing them to the RCC NASA Suits team, they will learn about Augmented Reality and how NASA is utilizing its many capabilities to further improve the way astronauts will work in many different environments that space has to offer. For parents who would like to stay with their child during this event they may also register on the Student/Parent Registration Form.

Middle School Students

During this event Middle School students will learn more about programming in C++ and, once they understand the basics, will take their newfound knowledge and solve basic coding problems. By doing these problems, the students will get an understanding on how real life problems are structured and given to programmers.

High School Students

High School students will put their knowledge of programming and problem solving to the test during our Tiger Competition. By competing in this event students will experience how problems will be presented in the work force, giving them an idea of what to expect in the future.
